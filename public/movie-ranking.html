Best movies according to SaGa~

My top 3 most favorite movies of all time

The Shawshank Redemption

I love The Shawshank Redemption because it shows how powerful hope can be, even in the darkest situations. I admire how Andy Dufresne never gives up, no matter how unfair or brutal life becomes. His quiet strength, intelligence, and patience inspire me. I feel deeply connected to the friendship between Andy and Red—it’s raw, real, and full of meaning. The ending gives me chills every time; it feels like the perfect reward for all the pain and struggle. This movie reminds me that even when everything seems lost, hope can set you free.

Schindler's List

I love Schindler’s List because it illustrates the strength of humanity in the face of unimaginable horror. I admire how Oskar Schindler transforms from a self-interested businessman into someone who risks everything to save innocent lives. His journey is deeply emotional and powerful. The film doesn’t shy away from the brutal truths of history, and that honesty makes it even more impactful. Every scene carries significant weight, and the moments of kindness amidst all the cruelty resonate with me long after the film ends. It serves as a reminder that even one person can make a difference, and that courage and compassion matter, even in the darkest times.

The Dark Knight

Batman has been an inspiration for me throughout my life. His ability to face challenges alone and remain ten steps ahead makes him stand out as more than just a superhero; he is a symbol of resilience and strategy. While I have always admired Batman, it was the Joker in The Dark Knight who left a lasting impression on me. Heath Ledger’s performance was haunting, chaotic, and brilliant; he brought a terrifying depth to the character that I couldn’t look away from. Christian Bale’s grounded and intense portrayal of Batman perfectly balanced that madness of Joker. With Christopher Nolan’s masterful direction tying it all together, the film became much more than just a comic book adaptation. It made me reflect on morality, choice, and the thin line between order and chaos..
